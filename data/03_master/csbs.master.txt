Computer Science and Business Systems
Department of Computer Science and Business Systems
The B.Tech in Computer Science and Business System (CSBS) is a specialized undergraduate
engineering program designed to blend core computer science education with insights into business systems
and management. It is a relatively new course introduced in collaboration with industry leaders like
Tata Consultancy Services (TCS) to bridge the gap between traditional computer science education and
the dynamic needs of the IT and business world.
This program aims to enhance the relevance of the Computer Science program in India
to meet the future demands of the IT industry whose landscape is rapidly changing in the era of
Business 4.0. The curriculum is designed to ensure that students should get better exposure to
emerging subjects such as Analytics, Deep learning, Machine Learning, Cloud Computing, Business Analytics,
Financial Management, Economics and Internet of Things and Design thinking and also to develop an equal
interest and appreciation towards humanity, value added science and other interrelated topics.
The students graduating from this program will be in high demand by the industries and will be productive.
To produce graduates who are not only technically sound in
computer science but also understand the fundamentals of business, economics, and management.
Core Computer Science subjects (Data Structures, Algorithms, OS, DBMS, AI/ML, etc.)
Business and Management subjects (Economics, Financial Management, Business Process Management, etc.)
Designed in partnership with TCS
Emphasis on employability and industry readiness
Problem-solving using computational thinking
Business analytics and decision-making
IT systems for business applications
TCS, Infosys, Wipro, Accenture, CTS. Startups and Fin Tech companies like FSS.
More interdisciplinary exposure
Better suited for roles in tech-enabled business functions
Greater alignment with industry expectations, especially in IT services and consulting.

Engineering Knowledge: Apply the knowledge of mathematics, science, engineering fundamentals, and an engineering specialization to the solution of complex engineering problems.
Problem Analysis:Identify, formulate, review research literature, and analyze complex engineering problems reaching substantiated conclusions using first principles of mathematics, natural sciences, and engineering sciences.
Design/Development Of Solutions: Design solutions for complex engineering problems and design system components or processes that meet the specified needs with appropriate consideration for the public health and safety, and the cultural, societal, and environmental considerations.
Conduct Investigations of Complex Problems:Use research-based knowledge and research methods including design of experiments, analysis and interpretation of data, and synthesis of the information to provide valid conclusions.
Modern Tool Usage:Create, select, and apply appropriate techniques, resources, and modern engineering and IT tools including prediction and modeling to complex engineering activities with an understanding of the limitations.
The Engineer and Society: Apply reasoning informed by the contextual knowledge to assess societal, health, safety, legal and cultural issues and the consequent responsibilities relevant to the professional engineering practice.
Environment and Sustain ability: Understand the impact of the professional engineering solutions in societal and environmental contexts, and demonstrate the knowledge of, and need for sustainable development.
Ethics: Apply ethical principles and commit to professional ethics and responsibilities and norms of the engineering practice.
Individual and Team Work: Function effectively as an individual, and as a member or leader in diverse teams, and in multi disciplinary settings.
Communication: Communicate effectively on complex engineering activities with the engineering community and with society at large, such as, being able to comprehend and write effective reports and design documentation, make effective presentations, and give and receive clear instructions.
Project Management and Finance: Demonstrate knowledge and understanding of the engineering and management principles and apply these to one's own work, as a member and leader in a team, to manage projects and in multi disciplinary environments.
Life-long Learning: Recognize the need for, and have the preparation and ability to engage in independent and life-long learning in the broadest context of technological change.
Employment Growth in Computer Science and Business Systems by 2025 – 2030 in India
The employment opportunities for professionals in Computer Science and Business Systems in India over
the next five years (2025–2030) will see significant growth, driven by the country’s increasing digitalization, rise
in e-commerce, adoption of AI, and the transformation of business processes. With India positioning itself as a hub
for both IT services and business innovation, there will be a strong demand for professionals who can blend technical
expertise with a solid understanding of business systems.
Key Drivers of Employment Growth in Computer Science and Business Systems (2025–2030)
Digital Transformation of Businesses
Business Process Automation: Companies across sectors are embracing automation, AI, cloud technologies, and big data to optimize their operations.
With the continued growth of Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP) systems like SAP, Oracle, and Microsoft Dynamics, and a shift towards Software as a Service (SaaS) models, professionals will be needed to implement, maintain, and integrate these solutions.
Growth of AI, Machine Learning, and Analytics
Data-Driven Decision Making: Businesses increasingly rely on data for decision-making, boosting demand for data analysts, data scientists, and business intelligence professionals who can transform raw data into actionable insights.
AI and ML in Business: With AI and machine learning at the heart of digital business transformations (such as customer relationship management, predictive analytics, and inventory management), professionals with expertise in these technologies will be highly sought after.
Cloud Computing and Digital Platforms
Cloud Migration: As businesses continue migrating to the cloud, there is a demand for cloud architects, cloud engineers, and business analysts who can help organizations seamlessly integrate cloud solutions into their operations.
Hybrid and Multi-Cloud Environments: The rise of multi-cloud strategies, using multiple providers like AWS, Azure, and Google Cloud, will require professionals who understand both the technical and business implications of such systems.
E-Commerce Growth and Customer Experience
E-commerce and Retail Transformation: With India's growing e-commerce market, businesses need professionals to build online platforms, optimize user experiences, and integrate AI-powered customer service tools like chatbots and recommendation systems.
Supply Chain Optimization: E-commerce companies will continue to invest in automated inventory management, logistics, and demand forecasting systems, creating demand for data scientists and systems analysts.
Enterprise Digitalization (FinTech, Healthcare, Manufacturing)
FinTech Expansion: As India’s fintech industry expands, there will be a rise in demand for professionals skilled in developing digital payment systems, blockchain technologies, and AI-powered financial services.
Healthcare Transformation: With healthcare systems adopting digital records, telemedicine, and AI for diagnostics, professionals with knowledge in both IT and business systems will be crucial.
Smart Manufacturing: With the adoption of Industry 4.0, IoT, and AI in manufacturing, businesses will need professionals to bridge the gap between technical systems and business operations.
Increased Cyber security Threats: As companies increasingly move operations online, the demand for professionals in cyber security (focused on protecting business systems) will rise. This includes roles in cyber defense, data protection, and ensuring compliance with data privacy regulations.
Regulatory Compliance: With evolving data protection laws (such as GDPR and India’s Personal Data Protection Bill), businesses will need experts to manage risk, privacy, and regulatory compliance.

Sectors with High Employment Potential in Computer Science and Business Systems
Information Technology (IT Services)
India’s IT services sector will continue to be a major driver of job opportunities in this field, including roles in cloud computing, business process outsourcing (BPO), and ERP implementation.
As India’s e-commerce market grows, there will be an increasing demand for professionals skilled in data analytics, customer experience design, and supply chain management.
The fintech sector is rapidly growing, offering opportunities in digital payments, blockchain, insurtech, and investment technology.
As healthcare systems embrace digital health, telemedicine, and AI for diagnostics, professionals with expertise in both business systems and IT will be in demand.
The smart manufacturing revolution (driven by IoT, AI, and cloud computing) will create roles focused on integrating IT systems with industrial processes.
