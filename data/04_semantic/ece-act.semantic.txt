SECTION: PROGRAMME OVERVIEW
The B.Tech programme in Electronics and Communication Engineering with Specialization in Advanced Communication Technology focuses on developing industry-competent professionals capable of addressing global challenges. The curriculum includes Wireless & Optical Communication, RF & Microwave Communication, 5G/6G systems, MIMO technologies, IoT, and their application in various sectors, such as healthcare and manufacturing.

SECTION: PROGRAMME DURATION AND ELIGIBILITY
The B.Tech programme has a duration of 4 years for regular students and 3 years for lateral entry students, divided into 8 and 6 semesters respectively. A total intake of 30 seats is available, with 15 seats allocated for government and management each. Eligibility requires a pass in Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics at the 10+2 level.

SECTION: PROGRAMME EDUCATIONAL OBJECTIVES (PEOs)
PEO1. Graduates will be prepared for analysing, designing, developing, and testing software solutions and products with creativity and sustainability. 
PEO2. Graduates will be skilled in the use of modern tools for critical problem-solving and analysing industrial and societal requirements. 
PEO3. Graduates will be prepared with managerial and leadership skills for their careers and for starting their own firms.

SECTION: PROGRAMME SPECIFIC OUTCOMES (PSOs)
PSO1. Graduates will develop creative solutions by adapting emerging technologies/tools for real-time applications in industry. 
PSO2. Graduates will apply the acquired knowledge to develop software solutions and innovative mobile apps for various automation applications.

SECTION: PROGRAMME OUTCOMES (POs)
PO1. Apply knowledge of mathematics, science, engineering fundamentals, and a specialization to solve complex engineering problems. 
PO2. Identify, formulate, and analyze complex engineering problems and reach substantiated conclusions. 
PO3. Design solutions for complex engineering problems considering public health, safety, and environmental factors. 
PO4. Conduct investigations of complex problems using research methods and provide valid conclusions. 
PO5. Create and apply modern engineering tools and techniques for complex activities, understanding their limitations. 
PO6. Apply contextual knowledge to assess societal, health, safety, legal, and cultural issues in professional engineering practice. 
PO7. Understand the impact of engineering solutions in societal contexts and demonstrate a need for sustainable development. 
PO8. Apply ethical principles and commit to professional ethics in engineering practices. 
PO9. Function effectively as an individual or as a team member/leader in diverse settings. 
PO10. Communicate effectively on complex engineering activities with varied audiences. 
PO11. Demonstrate knowledge and understanding of engineering and management principles to manage projects in multidisciplinary environments. 
PO12. Recognize the need for and prepare for lifelong learning amidst technological changes.

SECTION: JOB PROFILES
Design Engineer: To work on researching and designing new products, drafting, and testing prototypes, and ensuring smooth manufacturing processes. 
ASIC Engineer: To analyze system complexities and devise performance-enhancing solutions. 
Embedded Engineer: To design and implement software for embedded devices, participating in the software development life cycle. 
Network Support Engineer: To ensure smooth operation of communication systems within organizations. 
Software Engineer: To identify and develop new software programs that improve various sectors, including UI enhancements. 
Quality Analyst: To participate in the development and planning of test scripts and processes for various projects. 
Technician: To engage in initial software development phases, including prototyping and threat identification. 
Chief Technical Officer: To oversee an organization's technical sector at a managerial level, aligning technical efforts with company growth. 
ECE Engineering Consultant: To bridge the gap between clients and technical teams, ensuring compliance with industry regulations. 
Data Analyst: To analyze raw data to support decision-making and provide profitable solutions.

SECTION: SECTOR GROWTH PROJECTIONS (2025-2030)
Telecom (5G/6G): 10–12% yearly, requiring skills in RF engineering and network design. 
Satellite Communications: 12–15% yearly, focusing on satellite payload design and operations. 
IoT & Embedded Systems: 15–18% yearly, necessitating expertise in communication protocols. 
Broadband & Wireless: 8–10% yearly, focusing on optical fiber and Wi-Fi 6/6E technologies. 
R&D & Next-Gen Communications: 10–14% yearly, emphasizing quantum communication technologies.
