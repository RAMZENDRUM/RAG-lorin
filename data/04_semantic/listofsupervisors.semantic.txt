SECTION: Research and Development Objectives
The Research and Development Cell aims to nurture research culture in the College by promoting research in newly emerging and challenging areas of Engineering, Technology, Science, and Humanities. This includes encouraging students and faculty members to carry out research in these fields by providing necessary facilities and infrastructures required for conducting research. Additionally, the cell seeks to create zeal among the students and faculty towards research and innovation and to encourage them to publish their findings in conferences and journals. The cell assists students in applying for funding under student project schemes to various funding agencies like TNSCST, MSME, and IEI (I). It works closely with the industry to understand their needs, potentially resulting in new or improved products, processes, systems, or services that can increase company productivity while promoting Institute-Industry Collaboration. The cell supports the government in serving society by undertaking sponsored projects offered by agencies such as TNSCST, DST, AICTE, UGC, ISRO, DRDO, CSIR, and DBT. Furthermore, the cell enhances the general research capability of budding engineers by facilitating participation in conferences, seminars, workshops, and project competitions while organizing various workshops, seminars, and training sessions related to the promotion of research. Lastly, it encourages both students and faculty to apply for copyrights and patents as part of their research efforts.

SECTION: Activities and Events
A variety of activities are undertaken, including the Design of Flexible Pavement using IRC categorized as a Self-Driven Activity and Computer Architecture also categorized as a Self-Driven Activity. Virtual Workshops on Machine Learning, Online Workshops on Programming Skills, and a Technological Innovation and Academia Industry Collaboration under Self-Reliant India are similarly categorized. Further self-driven activities include FDP on Kinematics of Machinery, FDP on Teaching Engineering Graphics using ICT Tools, Virtual Workshop on Research Methodologies, and Basic Refrigeration and Air Conditioning. Additional self-driven activities focus on converting ideas to entrepreneurship. The schedule includes a National Webinar on Research, Innovation, and Ranking as a MIC driven activity, various IIC Calendar Activities featuring orientation sessions for all students and faculties conducted by Innovation Ambassador(s), a session on planning for Start-ups with legal and ethical steps, and an IIC Council Meeting for reviewing progress and planning. Workshops on Design Thinking, Awareness Programs on IPR, as well as an Internal Hackathon to select teams for SIH-2022 represent further initiatives.
