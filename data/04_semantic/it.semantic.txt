SECTION: PROGRAM OVERVIEW  
The role of Information Technology is vital in every aspect of human life. The field encompasses the study and use of systems for storing, retrieving, and processing information. With the introduction of computers, the business world has changed significantly, ensuring smooth operations and fostering faster responses to changes in global markets. Due to its development, globalization has increased, allowing for the swift sharing of information across the globe and facilitating easier and cheaper communication. Key fields within IT include Machine Learning, Deep Learning, Data Science, Artificial Intelligence, Cloud Computing, Networking, Mobile Computing, and Blockchain, which all present tremendous job openings.

SECTION: PROGRAMME DETAILS  
The B.Tech. in Information Technology has a duration of 4 years (Regular) and 3 years (Lateral Entry), covering 8 semesters for Regular students and 6 for Lateral Entry students. The total intake is 60 seats, divided into 30 for Government and 30 for Management. Eligibility requires students to complete the 10+2 education system, passing in Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics.

SECTION: VISION AND MISSION  
The vision is to become a center of excellence for transforming students into proficient IT professionals through sustainable practices. The mission includes imparting core fundamental knowledge and necessary skills in Information Technology, fostering critical thinking, ethics, lifelong learning, and creativity needed for the industry, and cultivating all-round competencies for career, higher education, and self-employability.

SECTION: PROGRAM EDUCATIONAL OBJECTIVES (PEOs)  
PEO1: Graduates will be prepared for designing and developing efficient Information Technology systems and software with creativity and sustainability.  
PEO2: Graduates will be skilled in the use of modern tools for critical problem solving and analyzing industrial and societal requirements.  
PEO3: Graduates will be prepared with managerial and leadership skills for career and starting up their own firms.

SECTION: PROGRAM SPECIFIC OUTCOMES (PSOs)  
PSO1: Graduates will apply the acquired knowledge and adapt modern tools to provide innovative solutions in Networking and Cyber Security applications.  
PSO2: Graduates will design, implement, and test mobile apps and software solutions for various automation applications.

SECTION: PROGRAM OUTCOMES (POs)  
PO1: Engineering Knowledge: Graduates will apply knowledge in mathematics, science, engineering fundamentals, and engineering specialization to the solution of complex engineering problems.  
PO2: Problem Analysis: Graduates will identify, formulate, review research literature, and analyze complex engineering problems using first principles of mathematics and natural sciences.  
PO3: Design/Development of Solutions: Graduates will design solutions for complex engineering problems, considering public health, safety, and cultural, societal, and environmental aspects.  
PO4: Conduct Investigations of Complex Problems: Graduates will utilize research-based knowledge and methods, including experimental design and data analysis, to synthesize information and provide valid conclusions.  
PO5: Modern Tool Usage: Graduates will select and apply appropriate techniques and modern engineering tools for complex engineering activities, understanding their limitations.  
PO6: The Engineer and Society: Graduates will apply reasoning informed by contextual knowledge to assess societal, health, safety, legal, and cultural issues relevant to professional engineering practice.  
PO7: Environment and Sustainability: Graduates will understand the impact of professional engineering solutions in societal and environmental contexts and demonstrate awareness of sustainable development.  
PO8: Ethics: Graduates will apply ethical principles and adhere to professional ethics and responsibilities in engineering practice.  
PO9: Individual and Team Work: Graduates will work effectively as individuals and as leaders or members of diverse teams in multidisciplinary settings.  
PO10: Communication: Graduates will communicate effectively on complex engineering activities, including writing reports and design documentation and making presentations.  
PO11: Project Management and Finance: Graduates will demonstrate knowledge of engineering and management principles and apply these as members and leaders in teams to manage projects in multidisciplinary environments.  
PO12: Life-long Learning: Graduates will recognize the need for and be prepared to engage in lifelong learning in the broadest context of technological change.
