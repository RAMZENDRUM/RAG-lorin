SECTION: Internal Quality Assurance Cell (IQAC)

The Internal Quality Assurance Cell (IQAC) of Mohamed Sathak A.J. College of Engineering plays a pivotal role in the institutional framework, adhering to the guidelines set forth by the National Assessment and Accreditation Council (NAAC) for effective performance evaluation, assessment, and accreditation. Its primary aim is to ensure continuous quality enhancement within the institution towards achieving academic excellence. The IQAC works as a facilitator to internalize quality culture, create mechanisms for institutional effectiveness, and uphold academic and administrative standards. The cell emphasizes participation from all constituents of the institution, ensuring that quality becomes a shared commitment rather than a mere administrative exercise.

SECTION: Objectives of IQAC

The IQAC aims to develop a systematic approach for consistent improvement in both academic and administrative performances of the institution. Key objectives include promoting internal quality culture and institutional best practices. Additionally, the IQAC focuses on creating mechanisms to ensure timely and efficient execution of academic and administrative tasks while maintaining the relevance and quality of various programs offered. 

SECTION: Functions of IQAC

Among the integral functions of the IQAC are the development and application of quality benchmarks for academic and administrative activities, the dissemination of information regarding quality parameters, the organization of workshops and seminars centered on quality improvement themes, and documentation of programs aimed at fostering enhancements in quality. The IQAC also acts as a nodal agency for quality-related activities, preparing the Annual Quality Assurance Report (AQAR) for submission to NAAC.

SECTION: Strategies of IQAC

The IGQAC implements strategies to ensure that academic performance is progressive and efficient. This involves optimizing modern teaching methods and verifying the adequacy and functionality of support structures such as libraries and laboratories. The cell is also responsible for establishing industry connections for research sharing and networking, thereby enhancing both local and global research collaborations.

SECTION: Criteria for Evaluation

The IQAC evaluates criteria across various domains such as curricular aspects, teaching-learning and evaluation processes, research, innovations, extension activities, infrastructure, student support, governance, and institutional values. Comprehensive assessments are conducted regularly to align institutional practices with quality standards mandated by NAAC, focusing on continuous improvement in education quality and institutional effectiveness.

SECTION: Conclusion

In pursuit of enhancing the academic and administrative landscape, the IQAC at Mohamed Sathak A.J. College of Engineering stands as a crucial element contributing to the institution's overall effectiveness and adherence to quality benchmarks. This collaborative mechanism aims to embed a quality culture that not only emphasizes compliance but actively promotes innovation and continuous growth across all levels of education.
