[SECTION: Overview]
Electronics and Communication Engineering as the name implies deals with the use of electronics concepts and principles to transfer information from one place to another. The field employs science and mathematical concepts to solve practical problems, fostering new products to enhance human life. The development of ICT has revolutionized areas such as health care, automation, remote sensing, and advanced communication.

[SECTION: Programme Details]
The B.E. in Electronics and Communication Engineering has a duration of 4 years for the regular program and 3 years for lateral entry, comprising 8 and 6 semesters respectively. The total intake is 60 seats, divided evenly between government and management quotas. Eligibility requires a 10+2 education with physics, chemistry, and mathematics.

[SECTION: PEOs]
The Programme Educational Objectives (PEOs) are as follows:
Graduates will be prepared for designing Electronics and Communication components and systems with creativity and sustainability. Graduates will be skilled in the use of modern tools for critical problem-solving and analyzing industrial and societal requirements. Graduates will be equipped with managerial and leadership skills for career advancement and entrepreneurship.

[SECTION: PSOs]
The Program Specific Outcomes (PSOs) state that Engineering Graduates will be able to apply the acquired knowledge to analyze, develop, and test creative solutions for Electronics and Communication Systems and provide cost-effective, eco-friendly solutions for real-time industrial and societal needs using Embedded and IoT tools.

[SECTION: POs]
The Programme Outcomes (POs) include the ability to apply knowledge of mathematics, science, and engineering fundamentals to complex problems. Students will demonstrate skills in problem analysis, design and development of solutions, conduct investigations of complex problems, and use modern tools for engineering practices. They will also apply ethical principles, work effectively as individuals and in teams, communicate effectively, manage projects, and recognize the need for lifelong learning in technological contexts.

[SECTION: Job Outlook]
The Job Outlook for Electronics & Communication Engineering (ECE) for the years 2025-2030 indicates promising opportunities influenced by major industry drivers such as 5G & 6G rollout, semiconductor and chip design, IoT, embedded systems, and advancements in AI and DSP.

[SECTION: Sector-Wise Job Growth Projections]
- Telecom (5G/6G): 10-12% per year in RF Design, Protocol Testing, and Antenna Development.
- Semiconductor/VLSI: 12-15% per year in areas such as RTL, Physical Design, DFT, ASIC/FPGA.
- Embedded & IoT: 15-18% per year focusing on C/ARM/RTOS, PCB, and Sensor Integration.
- Consumer Electronics: 8-10% per year in Firmware and Power Supply Design.
- Aerospace & Defence: 6-8% per year in Radar, Avionics, and Secure Communication.
- AI + DSP + Computer Vision: 10-14% per year in Image/Audio DSP and ML for signal processing.

[SECTION: Lab Facilities]
The following labs are available:
1. Communication Lab: Courses offered include EC8561 - Communication System and EC8761 - Advanced Communication.
2. DSP/VLSI Lab: Courses offered include EC8562 - Digital Signal Processing and EC8661 - VLSI Design.
3. Electronics Lab: Various courses including GE8261 - Engineering Practices and EC8462 - Linear Integrated Circuits.
4. Embedded Lab.
5. Microprocessors & Microcontrollers Lab: Courses include EC8681 - Microprocessors and Microcontrollers for both ECE and EEE.

[SECTION: Value Added Courses]
The value-added courses offered include LABVIEW, Embedded & IoT, Introduction to IoT (CISCO), Robotic Process Automation, and Amazon AWS Beginners, each with varying durations.
