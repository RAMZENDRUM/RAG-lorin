SECTION: Overview of MSAJCE and Cisco Partnership
Mohamed Sathak A. J. College of Engineering has partnered with CISCO, the world leaders in networking, to establish the Networking Academy (NetAcad). This collaboration aims to provide IT skills and career-building opportunities through the Cisco Networking Academy program.

SECTION: Course Curriculum and Learning Outcomes
Students will study the architecture, structure, functions, components, and models of the Internet and other computer networks. The curriculum utilizes the OSI and TCP layered models to examine the roles of protocols and services at the application, network, data link, and physical layers. Additionally, students will learn the principles of IP addressing and the fundamentals of Ethernet concepts, media, and operations, which provide a solid foundation for these studies. Labs utilize a "model Internet" to facilitate real data analysis without affecting production networks. Packet Tracer (PT) activities are designed to help students analyze protocol and network operations and build small networks in a simulated environment.

SECTION: Skills Development in Networking
The program enables students to build simple LANs, perform basic configurations for routers and switches, and implement IP addressing schemes. They will acquire the skills to configure and troubleshoot routers and switches, resolving common VLAN routing issues in both IPv4 and IPv6 networks. Students will also learn to configure WAN technologies and network services required by converged applications within a complex network.

SECTION: Enterprise Network Design
Students will gain knowledge to effectively use simulation tools for designing enterprise networks and evaluating design alternatives. They will complete designs that support applications like electronic mail, centralized database access, and client-server applications. Various issues such as IP address assignments, the selection of internetworking equipment, and network performance will be addressed in the design process.

SECTION: Hands-On Learning Experience
The program offers a unique hands-on learning experience in the latest networking, security, and cloud technologies using simulation tools. Students will obtain professional certification recognized by employers worldwide, opening career pathways to an estimated eight million networking jobs around the globe through NetAcad.

SECTION: Course Offerings
Courses offered in the program include Network Essentials, Mobility Fundamentals, CCNA: Introduction to Networks, CCNA: Switching, Routing, and Wireless Essentials, CCNA: Enterprise Networking, Security and Automation, Security Courses, IoT & Data Analytics Courses, OS and IT Courses, Programming Courses, and Entrepreneurship.

SECTION: Certifications Available
Certifications available through the program include CISCO Certified Network Associate and Implementing CISCO Enterprise Network Core Technologies.

SECTION: Lab Components
The program incorporates 24 hands-on and paper-based labs utilizing the Packet Tracer tool, and Cisco 4221 routers and 2960 switches. Total duration of these labs amounts to 70 hours, providing students with practical experience in networking.
