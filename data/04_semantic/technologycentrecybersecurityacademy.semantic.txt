SECTION: Cyber Security Academy Objectives and Outcomes
The students will be able to learn the general security concepts involved in maintaining a secure network computing environment, the nature and scope of today’s cybersecurity challenges, strategies for network defense, and next-generation cybersecurity solutions related to Essentials I & II. Cyber Security Foundation allows students to learn the fundamentals of cybersecurity, identifying the concepts required to recognize and potentially mitigate attacks against enterprise networks and mission-critical infrastructure. Gateway I & II teaches fundamental tenets of networking and concepts involved in maintaining a secure network computing environment.

SECTION: Cyber Security Curriculum
The students will be able to formulate an industry-standard design to protect infrastructure against cybersecurity threats through Essentials I, alongside applying advanced filtering methodologies such as user, application, and content ID to protect against known and unknown attack vectors. Cyber Security Foundation will enable students to apply the fundamentals of cybersecurity, recognizing and potentially mitigating attacks against enterprise networks and mission-critical infrastructure. Gateway I will have students demonstrate knowledge of interconnected technology in daily communication, identify systems that need protection, and apply physical, logical, and virtual addressing to various-sized networks via subnet mask schemes. Gateway II aims to examine cybersecurity landscape environments, attack threat vectors, exposure, vulnerabilities, and risk factors, while applying the knowledge and skills necessary to plan, design, implement, troubleshoot, and maintain network infrastructure environments. Essentials II will focus on demonstrating the ability to assess and harden endpoints based on security policies while utilizing advanced malware research and analysis for enhanced protection of enterprise networks.

SECTION: Unique Learning Opportunities
Students will experience a unique hands-on learning experience in the latest networking, security, and cloud technologies utilizing simulation tools. The program offers professional certifications recognized by employers worldwide and presents career pathways connecting to an estimated eight million networking jobs around the world through the Cisco Networking Academy.

SECTION: Courses Offered
The courses available include Network Essentials, Mobility Fundamentals, CCNA: Introduction to Networks, CCNA: Switching, Routing, and Wireless Essentials, CCNA: Enterprise Networking, Security and Automation, Security Courses, IoT & Data Analytics Courses, OS and IT Courses, Programming Courses, and Entrepreneurship.

SECTION: Certifications
Students have the opportunity to receive certifications such as Cisco Certified Network Associate and be trained in Implementing Cisco Enterprise Network Core Technologies. Their training includes Fundamentals of Cyber Security, which encompasses security trends, legal, ethical, and professional aspects of security, understanding the need for security at multiple levels, and familiarity with the OSI security architecture.

SECTION: Practical Lab Sessions
Lab sessions facilitate practical learning with exercises focusing on classical encryption techniques, such as substitution techniques, transposition techniques, and steganography. Students will delve into essential terminologies like CIA, Risks, Breaches, Threats, Attacks, Exploits, and information gathering methodologies, which include social engineering, foot printing, and scanning, during lab sessions using tools like nmap, zenmap, Port Scanners, and Network scanners. Security algorithms training will cover asymmetric key cryptography, message authentication, digital signatures, applications of cryptography, and an overview of various types of firewalls.

SECTION: Infrastructure and Malware Security
Infrastructure-based security training encompasses network packet sniffing, network design simulation, detecting DOS/DDOS attacks, asset management, audits, vulnerabilities, and intrinsic methods for intrusion detection and prevention systems. Lab sessions will involve implementing sniffing using tools, demonstrating intrusion detection systems, and exploring automated attack and penetration tools. Malware security covers definitions and classifications of malware, OS hardening techniques, and practical sessions on malware analysis and building Trojans.
