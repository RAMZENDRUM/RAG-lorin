SECTION: National Service Scheme (NSS) Overview  
The National Service Scheme (NSS) was established at Mohamed Sathak A. J. College of Engineering (MSAJCE), Chennai, in the year 2001 with the goal to uphold the need for selfless service and to encourage the spirit of social service among young students. Presently, the NSS unit of MSAJCE has over 100 active members from various disciplines of the first year and second year, working rigorously for the betterment of society in and around Chennai. The NSS unit has conducted various initiatives such as blood donation camps, awareness programs on 'Health and Hygiene,' Consumer Rights, Environmental Protection, and AIDS awareness.

SECTION: Objectives of NSS  
The objectives of the National Service Scheme (NSS) include to work for and among the people, to enhance the knowledge of themselves and the community, to apply their study to practical use in justifying at least some of the difficulties, to gain skills in the exercise of democratic leadership, to gain skills in program development to enable self-employment, to bridge the gap between the educated and the uneducated masses, and to promote the will to serve the weaker sections of the community.

SECTION: Duties of NSS Volunteers  
The duties of NSS volunteers include establishing relationships with society, identifying needs, problems, and resources of the community, and relating their learning experience towards finding solutions to identified problems. They must plan programs and execute them while systematically recording the activities and assessing progress periodically.

SECTION: Code of Conduct for NSS Volunteers  
The code of conduct for NSS volunteers requires that all volunteers work under the guidance of a group leader nominated by the program officer and make themselves worthy of the confidence and cooperation of community leadership. They should avoid entering into controversial issues, keep a day-to-day record of their activities/experience, and it is obligatory for every volunteer to wear the NSS badge while on work.

SECTION: National Importance Events  
The NSS organizes various events during national importance days such as National Youth Day, Independence Day, Republic Day, Road Safety Week, orphanage visits, polio drops programs, annual special camps, Swachh Bharat programs, orientation and inauguration of regular activities, blood donation camps, eye check-ups, dental camps, tree plantation programs, motivation programs for school students, voters/election awareness camps, and flood relief camps.

SECTION: Youth Red Cross (YRC) Overview  
The Youth Red Cross (YRC) was established at MSAJCE in the year 2014 and is an important constituent of the Indian Red Cross. It is a group movement organized for students to foster a spirit of social welfare and prepare young minds to contribute significantly to needy individuals with the principles of humanity, impartiality, neutrality, and voluntary service.

SECTION: Objectives of Youth Red Cross  
The objectives of the Youth Red Cross include conducting social and health awareness programs, raising awareness about personal and community health, encouraging students to offer humanitarian services, providing First Aid training to YRC volunteers, enabling the growth of a spirit of service and sense of duty among youth, and fostering better relationships without discrimination.

SECTION: YRC Recent Activities in 2022  
In the year 2022, YRC organized various events such as the Blood Donation Camp on 13.05.2022 at Right Hospitals, Royapet, the Eye Camp on 30.05.2022 for MSAJCE staff and students, the Free Medical & Dental Camp on 09.06.2022 for the community, the Mass Cleaning Camp on 10.08.2022, and the Sexual Harassment Awareness Programs on 11.08.2022 aimed at students from Government Higher Secondary Schools in Thiruporur and Kelampakam.

SECTION: Unnat Bharat Abhiyan (UBA) Overview  
Unnat Bharat Abhiyan (UBA) aims to promote rural development in alignment with Gandhian vision through localized solutions using decentralized, eco-friendly technologies. Its mission includes connecting higher education institutions with local communities to address the development challenges of rural India through participatory processes.

SECTION: UBA Recent Activities from 2019 to 2021  
In 2019, activities included various environmental and health-related initiatives such as the World Environmental Day Competition on 05.06.2019 conducted for Government higher secondary schools, tree plantation in Kaaranai Village on 21.08.2019, and a blood donation camp hosted for the general public on 29.08.2019. In 2020, the COVID Vaccination Camps were organized for the village people, adding significant value to community health initiatives.

SECTION: Key Events for Community Awareness  
Significant community awareness events have been recorded, including the Blind Walk on 12.10.2017 in Siruseri, the Aid India Training Programme on 06.10.2017 for the general public and students, the Educating Rural Child initiative focusing on providing food for rural school children, and various awareness campaigns tackling issues like dengue, traditional food awareness, and the campaign against plastic usage.

SECTION: Village Relationship Programs  
MSAJCE also undertakes village cleaning programs and health camps, such as the Medical Camp at Thalampur on 17.02.2017 and ongoing initiatives aimed at uplifting rural communities through clean-ups, educational programs, and health awareness workshops.
