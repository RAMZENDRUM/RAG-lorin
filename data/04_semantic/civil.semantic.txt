SECTION: PROGRAM OVERVIEW  
The Department of Civil Engineering at Mohamed Sathak A.J. College of Engineering, established in 2007, offers a B.E. in Civil Engineering over a duration of 4 years (8 semesters) for regular students and 3 years (6 semesters) for lateral entry students. The total intake is 30 seats, divided into 15 for government quota and 15 for management quota. Eligibility requires a pass in 10+2 with Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics.

SECTION: M.E. STRUCTURAL ENGINEERING  
The M.E. program in Structural Engineering spans 2 years and encompasses 4 semesters, with an intake of 18 students, following Anna University and AICTE norms for eligibility. The program focuses on advanced structural engineering concepts, allowing graduates to engage in higher studies or specialized roles in the industry.

SECTION: OBJECTIVES AND OUTCOMES  
The program aims to prepare graduates with the following educational objectives:
- PEO1: Graduates will be able to prepare design and detailing of civil engineering components and systems, with creativity and sustainability.
- PEO2: Graduates will be skilled in using modern tools for critical problem-solving and analysis of industrial and societal requirements.
- PEO3: Graduates will be prepared with managerial and leadership skills for career advancement or entrepreneurship.

The program specific outcomes include:
- PSO1: Graduates will apply their skills to projects in Urban/Rural Planning and development, providing environmentally friendly and sustainable solutions.
- PSO2: Graduates will design cost-effective structural components in various infrastructure development and civil engineering industries.

SECTION: PROGRAM OUTCOMES  
Graduates will attain the following outcomes:
- PO1: Apply knowledge of mathematics, science, and engineering fundamentals to solve complex engineering problems.
- PO2: Analyze complex engineering problems and draw substantiated conclusions using first principles.
- PO3: Design solutions for complex problems considering public health and safety as well as sociocultural and environmental factors.
- PO4: Conduct investigations on complex issues based on research knowledge and synthesis of information.
- PO5: Utilize modern engineering tools and techniques appropriately in engineering activities.
- PO6: Assess societal, health, safety, legal, and cultural issues related to professional engineering practice.
- PO7: Understand the impact of engineering solutions in societal and environmental contexts.
- PO8: Commit to ethical principles and professional responsibilities within engineering practices.
- PO9: Function effectively as individuals and as team members in diverse and multidisciplinary settings.
- PO10: Communicate effectively about complex engineering activities to the engineering community and society at large.
- PO11: Demonstrate knowledge of engineering management principles to manage projects effectively.
- PO12: Recognize the importance of lifelong learning in response to technological changes.

SECTION: JOB MARKET AND PROJECTIONS  
The employment outlook for civil engineering in India from 2025 to 2030 shows varied growth across sectors:
- Public Infrastructure (Govt.): 6–8% per year, driven by large national projects.
- Real Estate & Housing: 4–6% per year due to urbanization and housing demand.
- Transportation (roads/rails): 7–10% per year, buoyed by investments in national highways.
- Green & Sustainable Engineering: 10–12% per year, showcasing a high potential in emerging fields.
- BIM & Smart Construction: Over 15% annually, reflecting a rapid growth trend.
