- [SECTION: Overview]
Mechanical engineering is the oldest and second largest engineering discipline. This field provides a solid foundation for professional engineering practices and involves the design, development, manufacture, and operation of many types of plants, machinery, and mechanical products. The study develops students' ability to apply scientific principles in the design and analysis of mechanical and energy conversion systems. Students face design challenges in most major courses, which fosters creativity in solving real-world issues. Emerging areas like robotics, cryogenic engineering, composite materials, nano materials and technology, and renewable energy resources promise tremendous growth in the future. Students will create innovative solutions to real-world problems, demonstrating good practical skills using science and math, and essential organizing, practicing, and interpersonal skills suitable for manufacturing environments.

- [SECTION: Programme Structure]
The B.E. in Mechanical Engineering lasts for four years (Regular) or three years (Lateral Entry), comprising eight semesters for regular students and six for lateral entry students, with a total intake of 60 seats (30 for Government and 30 for Management). Students must have completed the 10+2 education system with a pass in Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics to be eligible.

- [SECTION: Educational Objectives]
The program's objectives include:
PEO1: Graduates will be prepared for designing mechanical components and systems, focusing on creativity and sustainability.
PEO2: Graduates will be skilled in using modern tools for critical problem-solving, analyzing industrial and societal needs.
PEO3: Graduates will develop managerial and leadership skills, enabling them to start their own firms and advance their careers.

- [SECTION: Specific Outcomes]
Program Specific Outcomes (PSO) include:
PSO1: Apply acquired mechanical engineering knowledge to solve industrial problems effectively using modern tools.
PSO2: Develop cost-effective and eco-friendly mechanical components and systems required in industry and society.

- [SECTION: Programme Outcomes]
The Programme Outcomes (PO) include:
PO1: Engineering Knowledge: Application of mathematics, science, and engineering fundamentals to solve complex engineering problems.
PO2: Problem Analysis: Identification and formulation of complex problems, analyzing them through research literature and mathematical principles.
PO3: Design/Development of Solutions: Engineering solutions that meet specified needs while considering health, safety, cultural, and environmental factors.
PO4: Conduct Investigations of Complex Problems: Utilization of research-based knowledge to provide valid conclusions through experiments and data analysis.
PO5: Modern Tool Usage: Effective creation and application of modern engineering techniques and IT tools to complex activities.
PO6: The Engineer and Society: Informed reasoning to address societal, health, safety, and legal issues in professional practice.
PO7: Environment and Sustainability: Understanding the societal and environmental impacts of professional engineering solutions and the need for sustainable development.
PO8: Ethics: Commitment to ethical principles and responsibilities in engineering practice.
PO9: Individual and Team Work: Functioning effectively both individually and as part of diverse teams in multidisciplinary settings.
PO10: Communication: Effective communication regarding complex engineering activities with both the engineering community and society.
PO11: Project Management and Finance: Application of engineering and management principles in a team environment.
PO12: Life-long Learning: Recognition of the need for lifelong learning in the context of technological changes.
