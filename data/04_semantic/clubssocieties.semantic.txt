SECTION: Sports Club  
The Sports Club at Mohamed Sathak A. J. College of Engineering aims to build up sportsmanship, foster intimate friendships, reduce stress and improve health, improve skills and enhance employability, support in developing a positive attitude, self-confidence, courage, and patience, provide opportunities to see the community closely and gain experience of human nature in relation to their environment, and avail the opportunity to meet people from different walks of life. Outdoor games include Cricket, Football, Volleyball, Basketball, Rugby, Badminton, Kho Kho, and Throughball, while Indoor games include Carrom, Table Tennis, and Chess.

SECTION: Fine Arts Club  
The Fine Arts Club at Mohamed Sathak A. J. College of Engineering was successfully inaugurated to encourage the budding talents of students. S. Viswajaa (Assistant Professor - IT) serves as the Staff Co-ordinator of the Fine Arts Club - Artful Aesthetics and emphasizes the importance of this club in keeping students involved throughout the year. The club aims to inspire and enable students to participate actively and develop their skills such as Dance, Art, Singing, Music, and Acting. Various competitions, functions, and events are organized to help students express their thoughts, feelings, and creativity through visual art forms. The Student President is Kishore P - IV Year Mechanical, and the Vice-President is Laksha - III Year CSE.

SECTION: Science Club  
The Science Club was established to motivate students through interactions with eminent scientists and to introduce them to exciting science and technology projects. The club serves to promote interest, understanding, and knowledge of the scientific world among students and the local community. Divided into Physics, Chemistry, and General sections, the Science Club strives to augment students' interest in science and promote academic achievement in scientific subjects. Its activities include experiments like making electronic components, invisible ink, electroplating, biodiesels, fire writing, and venation of leaves, with opportunities for hands-on participation, collaboration, and presentations. 

SECTION: Tamil Mandram  
The Tamil Mandram emphasizes the importance of preserving the Tamil language and culture, promoting interest and knowledge of Tamil among students. Its aims include fostering enthusiasm for Tamil literature, ancient Tamil culture, and technology awareness in the Tamil language. The club hosts various cultural events to engage students and promote community involvement.

SECTION: Coding Club  
The Coding Club focuses on enhancing programming abilities among students through coding contests, workshops on topics such as web development, machine learning, and IoT, and participation in intercollegiate competitions like ACM-ICPC and Google Code Jam. The club meets weekly to discuss ongoing projects and prepare members for internships and job placements through seminars and workshops by seniors and former members.

SECTION: Robotic Club  
The Robotic Club serves as a platform for students interested in robotics, encouraging the design and construction of robots for various competitions. The club aims to instill a mindset of technological thinking, enabling students to creatively apply their knowledge and develop professional engineering skills beneficial for their future careers. Activities include robotics education initiatives and workshops.

SECTION: Energy & Eco Club  
The Energy Club aims to raise awareness about responsible energy use and promote conservation, while the Eco Club empowers students to take action in upholding environmental health and preventing degradation. These clubs introduce students to energy conservation concepts and foster a sense of responsibility towards environmental issues through various programs and initiatives.

SECTION: Photography Club  
The Photography Club encourages students to explore their interest in photography, filmmaking, and screenwriting. It provides a platform for members to develop their skills in taking and editing photographs while learning about composition and creative expression. Students are motivated to capture moments related to college events and activities, regardless of their skill level.
