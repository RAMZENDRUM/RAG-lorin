SECTION: Notes on Best Practices
Best Practice 1: 
Title: Technology Centres
Mohamed Sathak A.J. College of Engineering (MSAJCE) was established to be an eminent institute for higher education and research through innovative teaching and sustainable practices. This vision is fulfilled by providing holistic, multi-disciplinary skill-based education leading to innovation and entrepreneurship. The training activities split into academic forenoon sessions and afternoon training practices within regular hours.

Best Practice 2: 
Title: Afternoon Laboratory Classes
The institution conducts all laboratory classes in the afternoon, ensuring students do not fatigue between lectures. This practice is complemented by inviting industry experts to enhance learning experiences in real-world applications.

SECTION: Code of Conduct
The code serves as a guide to the expected responsibilities and ethical behavior for students and staff at MSAJCE, emphasizing integrity, discipline, and adherence to institutional goals. Students are required to maintain decorum, punctuality, submit assignments on time, and adhere to dress codes and attendance norms. Faculty members must also ensure decorum in classrooms and uphold discipline among students. Non-teaching staff are to maintain operational efficiency and provide safety and security during lab classes. Administrative staff are required to observe professional ethics and respond assistively to students and faculty needs.
