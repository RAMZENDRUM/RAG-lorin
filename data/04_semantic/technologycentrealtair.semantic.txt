SECTION: Technology Centre Objectives  
The students will be able to understand and interpret drawings of machine components. The students will also prepare assembly drawings using standard CAD packages. Furthermore, they will gain practical experience in handling 2D drafting and 3D modeling software systems. In addition, they will comprehend, design, analyze, and suggest novel materials, products, and solutions for contemporary manufacturing issues.

SECTION: CAD Model Development  
The students will develop CAD models for hypermesh. They will import and export CAD data and generate .hm files. The students will also attain accurate and high-quality meshes in 1D, 2D, and 3D elements. Additionally, they will carry out Linear Static Analysis and Dynamic Analysis.

SECTION: Networking Academy  
Mohamed Sathak A J College of Engineering has partnered with CISCO, the world leaders in networking, to establish the Networking Academy (NetAcad). CISCO Networking Academy is a career-building program that provides unique hands-on learning experiences in the latest networking, security, and cloud technologies using simulation tools. This program offers professional certification recognized by employers worldwide and career pathways to connect to an estimated eight million networking jobs around the world.

SECTION: Courses Offered  
The following courses are offered through the Networking Academy: Network Essentials, Mobility Fundamentals, CCNA: Introduction to Networks, CCNA: Switching, Routing, and Wireless Essentials, CCNA: Enterprise Networking, Security, and Automation or Networking Courses, Security Courses, IoT & Data Analytics Courses, OS and IT Courses, Programming Courses, and Entrepreneurship.

SECTION: Certifications  
Students who complete the program can become CISCO Certified Network Associates. They can also gain skills in implementing CISCO Enterprise Network Core Technologies.
