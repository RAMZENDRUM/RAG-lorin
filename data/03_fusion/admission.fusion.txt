[SECTION: Admission Overview]
Mohamed Sathak A. J. College of Engineering (MSAJCE) offers various undergraduate, postgraduate, and research programs in engineering. The undergraduate programs include Civil, Computer Science, Electronics, and Mechanical Engineering, with a total of 60 sanctioned seats for Computer Science & Engineering. Additionally, 5% of seats are reserved for NRI candidates. Students can apply through the Tamil Nadu Engineering Admissions (TNEA) counseling process.

[SECTION: Undergraduate Programmes]
The undergraduate programs at MSAJCE include Civil Engineering (30 seats), Computer Science & Engineering (60 seats), and Mechanical Engineering (30 seats). Each program has equitable distribution between Government and Management Quota, ensuring accessibility to diverse applicants. Specific admission guidelines emphasize eligibility based on HSC (Academic) or equivalent performance in relevant subjects.

[SECTION: Postgraduate Programmes and Eligibility]
MSAJCE offers postgraduate degrees such as M.E. in Computer Science & Engineering and Structural Engineering, with an intake of 9 and 18 seats, respectively. Admission is governed by TANCET scores and specific qualifications ensuring that only qualified candidates secure their places.

[SECTION: Research Opportunities]
The college provides Ph.D. programs specifically in Mechanical Engineering. Candidates must have a Master's degree in a relevant field along with substantial research experience, demonstrating the institution's commitment to fostering advanced academic pursuits and contributions to engineering research.

[SECTION: Scholarships and Financial Aid]
MSAJCE offers various scholarships, including the Pragati Scholarship for girl students and the Saksham Scholarship for differently-abled individuals, emphasizing financial support for deserving candidates. Each scholarship program requires specific eligibility criteria, ensuring that assistance is directed to those in genuine need.

[SECTION: Skill Development Courses]
The college offers skill development courses in sectors such as Computer Science and Mechanical Engineering, facilitating practical training for students. Approved intakes include 30 candidates for machine learning and refrigeration technician roles, aligning educational offerings with job market demands, and enhancing employability.
