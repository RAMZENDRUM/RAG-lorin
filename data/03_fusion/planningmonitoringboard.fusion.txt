[SECTION: Leadership of the Planning and Monitoring Board]
The Planning and Monitoring Board at Mohamed Sathak A. J. College of Engineering (MSAJCE) is chaired by Mr. Mohamed Sathak, the Campus Director from Mohamed Sathak Trust, Chennai. Supporting him as the Member Secretary is the Principal of MSAJCE. The board consists of expert advisors including Dr. K.S. Badri Nathan, Dean of Educational Development at Sri Venkateswara College of Engineering, and Mr. Rajesh Ramanujam, General Manager at Ford Credit IT. Other advisors represent various sectors, contributing to the board's diversity and expertise.

[SECTION: Key Responsibilities of the Planning and Monitoring Board]
The Planning and Monitoring Board at MSAJCE oversees critical academic and administrative functions, ensuring compliance with educational standards. This body is responsible for evaluating programs and monitoring academic performance, while also addressing grievances through the dedicated Grievance and Redressal Committee. The board aims to enhance educational quality through the implementation of effective policies and maintaining institutional distinctiveness.

[SECTION: MSAJCE's Commitment to Students]
Mohamed Sathak A. J. College of Engineering demonstrates a strong commitment to its students through comprehensive support systems, including a dedicated Grievance Cell and a Student Affairs department. The college promotes academic excellence by providing resources such as an online application form and fee payment options, facilitating easy access to education. In addition, initiatives like workshops and internship opportunities aim to prepare students effectively for their careers in engineering.

[SECTION: Academic Calendar and Events]
MSAJCE hosts significant events throughout the academic year, including the 18th Graduation Day scheduled for February 22, 2025, and various Faculty Development Programs like the AICTE-ATAL Sponsored six-day workshop. These events not only celebrate student achievements but also enhance academic growth and community engagement within the college.
