[SECTION: Anti-Ragging Policy Overview]
Ragging at Mohamed Sathak A. J. College of Engineering (MSAJCE) is strictly prohibited, aimed at creating a safe and supportive environment for students. The college emphasizes the collective responsibility of management, faculty, staff, and students to achieve a "ragging-free" institution. This aligns with UGC regulations, which define ragging as any act of teasing, handling rudely, or causing fear among freshers and other students.

[SECTION: Consequences of Ragging]
The consequences for engaging in ragging at MSAJCE are severe and include suspension from classes, withdrawal of scholarships, and potential expulsion from the institution. Each incident will be investigated by the Anti-Ragging Squad, which submits findings to the Principal to determine the appropriate actions based on the gravity of the offense.

[SECTION: Anti-Ragging Committee Members]
The Anti-Ragging Committee at MSAJCE includes key members to oversee cases effectively. This team is led by Dr. K.S. Srinivasan, the Principal, with Dr. S. Devikala as the Head of Student Affairs, alongside dedicated members like Dr. A. Balakrishnan and Mr. V.Vigneshwaran, ensuring comprehensive oversight and response to any incidents of ragging.

[SECTION: Definition of Ragging]
According to UGC, ragging includes behaviors like teasing freshers, disrupting academic activities, and any form of financial extortion or physical abuse. Such actions negatively impact a student's mental health and self-esteem, and the college encourages a nurturing atmosphere to prevent these acts, ensuring freshers can adapt to college life confidently.
