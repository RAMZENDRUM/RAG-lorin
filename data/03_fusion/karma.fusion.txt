[SECTION: KAUSHAL AUGMENTATION AND RESTRUCTURING MISSION (KARMA)]
The Kaushal Augmentation and Restructuring Mission of AICTE (KARMA) aims to address the shortage of skilled labor in India by implementing strategic skill initiatives. This program encourages institutions to optimize their existing higher education infrastructure for skill training, realigning resources to ensure training aligns with National Skills Qualifications Framework (NSQF). Candidates who have completed at least the 10th standard or have ITI qualifications are eligible for this domain-specific training, designed to enhance employability in core engineering sectors.

[SECTION: SKILLING MODELS UNDER KARMA]
KARMA proposes three distinct models to enhance skill development among students. Model 1 targets school dropouts post-10th grade, while Model 2 focuses on upgrading the skills of students currently enrolled in AICTE-approved technical institutions. This model introduces advanced skilling courses from the 4th to 7th semesters to prepare students for industry demands. Lastly, Model 3 integrates school education with higher education by initiating weekly skill-based training for nearby school students, fostering collaboration between higher education institutions and schools.

[SECTION: APPROVED COURSES]
The Mohamed Sathak A. J. College of Engineering has been authorized by AICTE to offer five courses under Model 2, including: AI - Machine Learning Developer, Refrigeration and Air Conditioning Technician, Additive Manufacturing Technician (3D Printing), Certificate Course in Embedded System Design using ARM/Cortex Microcontroller, and Architectural Drafting with Autodesk Revit. Under Model 1, the institute is also approved to offer Computer Hardware Network Maintenance and Refrigeration and Air Conditioning Technician courses, both with a maximum intake of 30 students.
