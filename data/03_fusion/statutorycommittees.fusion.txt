[SECTION: Statutory Committees]
Mohamed Sathak A. J. College of Engineering (MSAJCE) has established various statutory committees to support governance and student welfare. These include the Grievance and Redressal Committee, Women's Empowerment Cell, and the Entrepreneurship Development Cell. Each committee plays a crucial role in maintaining an inclusive and supportive environment, ensuring adherence to educational standards and addressing student concerns effectively. 

[SECTION: Academic Regulation and Policies]
MSAJCE adheres to strict academic regulations, ensuring quality education through clearly defined curricula and syllabi. The college is recognized by Anna University and is compliant with AICTE standards, promoting a transparent educational environment. These regulations serve to enhance learning and uphold institutional integrity throughout student admission and progress.

[SECTION: Annual Graduation Days]
MSAJCE conducts its Graduation Day annually, with the next event scheduled for February 22, 2025. This ceremony celebrates student achievements by recognizing graduating students’ accomplishments in engineering and technology. Notable among previous ceremonies are the 17th and 18th Graduation Days, which foster a sense of community and pride among students, faculty, and family members.

[SECTION: Support and Student Services]
The college offers various support services for students, including a dedicated Grievance Cell and resources for academic assistance. MSAJCE emphasizes student engagement through various clubs and professional societies, enhancing both academic and social experiences. The institution is committed to fostering student success through continuous feedback mechanisms and support systems.
