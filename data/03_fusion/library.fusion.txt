[SECTION: Library Overview]
The Library at Mohamed Sathak A. J. College of Engineering is a pivotal resource, spanning 8,978 square feet and fully computerized. It houses over 29,853 volumes, including 1,885 reference books, ensuring students and staff have access to a wide array of scholarly resources. The library subscribes to approximately 35 national journals, 20 magazines, and features 3,790 e-books, fulfilling the informational needs of its academic community. Key aspects include operational hours from 8 AM to 7 PM weekdays and 10 AM to 4 PM on Sundays.

[SECTION: Library Resources]
The library's resources are extensive, featuring a total of 29,853 books, which include various categories such as e-journals (1,379), CD-ROMs (356), and project reports (260). It also offers access to numerous databases, including DELNET and J-Gate, enhancing the research capabilities of students and faculty alike. Regular updates ensure new titles and volumes are consistently added to cater to the evolving academic environments. 

[SECTION: Membership and Borrowing Policies]
All students and staff at MSAJCE are library members, with borrowing entitlements set as follows: UG and PG students can borrow up to 18 books for 30 days, while teaching staff can borrow 10 books. Regulations include penalties for overdue books, emphasizing the importance of returning materials promptly. This structured approach promotes responsible library use amongst the academic community.

[SECTION: Library Committee]
The Library Committee at MSAJCE, chaired by Dr. K.S. Srinivasan, Principal, is composed of various faculty members and student representatives. This diverse group is responsible for overseeing library operations and ensuring it meets the academic and research needs of the institution. The committee includes key positions such as the Library In-Charge, Dr. Kamalaselvan A., and Chief Librarian, Mr. S. Sudhakar, among other faculty and student representatives.

[SECTION: E-Learning Resources]
MSAJCE provides students with additional e-learning resources, leveraging platforms like NPTEL, Coursera, and MIT OpenCourseWare. These platforms cater to varied learning preferences and reinforce classroom teachings, allowing students to access a wealth of knowledge online. This investment in digital resources reflects the college's commitment to enhancing educational experiences through technology integration.
