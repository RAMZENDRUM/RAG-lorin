[SECTION: Overview of Civil Engineering at MSAJCE]
The Civil Engineering Department at Mohamed Sathak A.J. College of Engineering (MSAJCE), established in 2007, focuses on creating proficient civil engineers through innovative teaching and sustainable practices. With programs like B.E. in Civil Engineering and M.E. in Structural Engineering, the department prepares students for diverse roles in urban/rural planning, construction, and environmental engineering. The curriculum is designed to foster critical thinking, ethical practices, and lifelong learning needed in today's engineering landscape.

[SECTION: Graduation Details and Opportunities]
The B.E. Civil Engineering program runs for four years with an intake capacity of 30 students, while the M.E. Structural Engineering program lasts two years with 18 seats. Graduates are well-prepared for innovative careers as planners, designers, and environment stewards. The projected job growth for civil engineers is promising, driven by governmental infrastructure investments and urbanization trends, with an anticipated annual growth of 6–8%.

[SECTION: Faculty Expertise and Contributions]
The department boasts a strong faculty, including Dr. Mohamed Adil Haque (Head - PG), who specializes in Structural Engineering, and Dr. Someswaran R (Head - UG), an expert in Water Resources Engineering. The faculty's diverse specializations enrich the academic environment and support research and industry collaborations. Their commitment to high professional standards equips graduates with necessary skills and insights for successful careers in engineering.

[SECTION: Program Educational Objectives and Outcomes]
The Civil Engineering curriculum is structured around specific objectives and outcomes that ensure graduates can design sustainable engineering solutions, analyze complex problems, and communicate effectively. The objectives include developing managerial and leadership skills, utilizing modern tools for problem-solving, and preparing for entrepreneurial ventures in civil engineering.

[SECTION: Job Profiles and Market Insights]
Civil Engineering graduates from MSAJCE have a wide range of job opportunities in government sectors and leading construction firms such as L&T and Shapoorji Pallonji. Entry-level roles include Site Engineer and Junior Design Engineer. The employment outlook indicates a job growth of approximately 5–6% annually for graduates, increasing with the acquisition of specialized skills in emerging technologies.
