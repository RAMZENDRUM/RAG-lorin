[SECTION: Entrepreneurship Development Cell]
The Entrepreneurship Development Cell at Mohamed Sathak A.J. College of Engineering (MSAJCE) plays a crucial role in fostering innovation and entrepreneurship among students. Led by Dr. E. Dhiravidachelvi, a Professor in Electrical and Electronics Engineering, this cell conducts various initiatives including workshops and mentorship programs. Its objective is to empower students to transform ideas into viable business ventures, supported by various members including legal consultant Mrs. Adhilakshmi Logamurthy and experts from NGOs. This collaborative environment encourages a culture of entrepreneurship while integrating academic knowledge.

[SECTION: Leadership and Membership]
The Entrepreneurship Development Cell comprises a diverse team dedicated to nurturing entrepreneurial skills. Key members include Dr. I. Manju, a Professor in Electrical and Electronics Engineering, and Ms. S. Usha, an Assistant Professor in English. Other contributors include professionals from diverse sectors, like Mr. Hussain Basha and Dr. Ushaa Eswaran, ensuring a well-rounded mentorship experience. The collaborative effort combines academic and practical insights to guide aspiring entrepreneurs towards success, with support also coming from parents involved in the initiative.

[SECTION: Key Initiatives and Programs]
MSAJCE's Entrepreneurship Development Cell offers several programs to enhance student engagement in entrepreneurial activities. The cell organizes workshops, guest lectures, and interactive sessions designed to instill practical skills and business knowledge. Additionally, it facilitates partnerships with local businesses and NGOs to provide real-world experience. This initiative not only prepares students for future careers but also significantly contributes to the local economy by fostering innovation and startup culture.
