[SECTION: 3D Printing Technology Center Overview]
The Centre for 3D Printing and Additive Manufacturing at Mohamed Sathak A. J. College of Engineering focuses on equipping students with advanced knowledge and skills in 3D printing technologies. The curriculum covers essential aspects such as material selection, equipment, and techniques vital for developing products in an Industry 4.0 environment, emphasizing interdisciplinary applications in sectors like aerospace and healthcare.

[SECTION: Hands-On Learning and Practical Applications]
Students are engaged in hands-on learning experiences that include developing CAD models for 3D printing, importing and exporting CAD data, and generating .stl files for manufacturing. They will learn to select appropriate materials and 3D printing processes to produce innovative products using Additive Manufacturing techniques.

[SECTION: Cisco Networking Academy Partnership]
Mohamed Sathak A. J. College of Engineering has established a partnership with Cisco to create the Networking Academy (NetAcad), providing students with extensive training in networking, security, and cloud technologies. This program offers professional certifications recognized globally, thereby enhancing career pathways to access millions of networking jobs worldwide.

[SECTION: Courses Offered in Networking]
The Networking Academy provides a diverse curriculum, featuring courses such as Network Essentials, CCNA: Introduction to Networks, and IoT & Data Analytics Courses. These courses are designed to prepare students for evolving careers in technology and ensure they acquire skills necessary for modern IT challenges.

[SECTION: Additive Manufacturing Academic Goals]
The course objectives for the Additive Manufacturing program include understanding various software tools and techniques for digital manufacturing. Students will learn to apply these techniques through practical exercises that culminate in the production of 3D-printed products, enhancing both theoretical and practical knowledge.

[SECTION: Suggested Readings for Further Learning]
To complement the 3D printing curriculum, students are encouraged to explore recommended texts, including "Additive Manufacturing Technologies" by Lan Gibson and "Understanding Additive Manufacturing" by Andreas Gebhardt. These resources provide in-depth insights into the fundamental principles and applications of additive manufacturing technologies.
